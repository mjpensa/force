### Embedded Finance and Banking-as-a-Service Revolution

Traditional banking is being disaggregated as non-bank companies embed financial services directly into customer experiences. Shopify launched Shopify Balance business accounts for 2.1 million merchants. Uber offers instant payouts via Uber Money. Tesla provides auto financing through Tesla Finance.

**Market Size:**
Embedded finance market: $138 billion globally in 2024, projected $622 billion by 2029 (Bain & Company). North America represents $47 billion current market.

**Banking-as-a-Service (BaaS) Platforms:**
- **Stripe Treasury**: Enables any platform to offer banking services, processes $640B annually, 100+ platforms integrated
- **Synapse** (bankrupt 2024): Cautionary tale - poor compliance, lost $85M in customer deposits
- **Unit.co**: 40,000+ companies using their BaaS infrastructure, $127M Series B at $1.2B valuation
- **Treasury Prime**: Bank-agnostic middleware, integrated with 15 FDIC-insured banks

**Revenue Models:**
- Interchange fees: $0.15-$0.30 per debit transaction
- Account fees: $3-$8 per active account monthly
- Lending revenue share: 15-30% of interest income
- Payment processing: 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction

**Regulatory Challenges:**
- OCC issued guidance in July 2024 on third-party risk management for bank-fintech partnerships
- FDIC increased scrutiny after Synapse collapse - requires detailed documentation of customer fund flows
- State money transmission licenses: $2M-$5M investment, 12-24 month timeline for all 50 states

**Competitive Dynamics:**
- 67% of consumers would trust brand more than bank for financial services (Plaid survey)
- Traditional banks losing $18B annually in revenue to embedded finance (Accenture)
- But: Only 23% of embedded finance startups are profitable (high customer acquisition costs, regulatory expenses)

**Strategic Imperatives for Banks:**
- Partner: Become BaaS infrastructure provider for platforms
- Compete: Launch own embedded products for business customers
- Acquire: Buy fintech companies with distribution (JPMorgan acquired 75% of Volkswagen payments unit)
- Risk: Doing nothing = commoditization of banking services, relegated to balance sheet provider role
