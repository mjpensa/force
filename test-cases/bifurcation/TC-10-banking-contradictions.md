# Small Business Lending Platform - Conflicting Research

## Market Size Analysis (Source 1 - McKinsey 2025)

U.S. small business lending market is $780 billion annually with 28.8 million small businesses. Digital lending represents 34% of originations, growing at 18.4% CAGR.

## Market Size Analysis (Source 2 - Federal Reserve 2024)

Small business lending market is $1.2 trillion annually. Traditional bank lending represents 71% of market. Digital lending growing at 12.1% CAGR.

**NOTE: Conflicting data on market size and digital lending penetration**

## Customer Demand (Source 1 - Biz2Credit Survey)

72% of small business owners prefer digital loan applications. Average time to decision expectation: 24 hours or less. Only 23% willing to wait more than 48 hours.

## Customer Demand (Source 2 - NFIB Small Business Report)

58% of small business owners prefer in-person relationship banking. 41% cite "trust concerns" with fully digital lenders. Average acceptable timeline: 3-5 business days.

**NOTE: Contradictory preferences - digital vs. traditional**

## Default Rate Projections (Source 1 - Lending Analytics Firm)

Small business loan default rates: 6.8% for digital lenders, 4.2% for traditional banks. Higher risk stems from automated underwriting and less stringent criteria.

## Default Rate Projections (Source 2 - Bank Industry Data)

Digital lenders report lower default rates (4.7%) than traditional banks (5.9%) due to superior AI-powered risk models and real-time data analysis.

**NOTE: Complete contradiction on which channel has better default rates**

## Technology Investment (Source 1)

Building competitive digital lending platform requires $18-25 million in technology investment over 2 years.

## Technology Investment (Source 2)

Modern cloud-based lending platforms can be deployed for $3-5 million using white-label solutions and SaaS tools.

**NOTE: 5x difference in cost estimates**

## Recommended Approach

Given contradictory research, recommend:
1. Commission independent market research study ($125K, 8-week timeline)
2. Run pilot program with limited capital ($10M loan portfolio)
3. A/B test digital-only vs. relationship-banker-assisted origination
4. Measure actual default rates, customer satisfaction, unit economics before scaling
5. Timeline: 6-month pilot before $50M+ commitment decision
