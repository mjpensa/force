# Branch Network Optimization Strategy

## Executive Summary

Regional bank operates 127 physical branches across 8 states. Customer foot traffic declined 47% since 2019 while digital banking transactions increased 312%. Strategic review recommends closing 34 underperforming branches, renovating 18 high-traffic locations, and opening 8 new flagship branches in growth markets.

## Branch Performance Analysis

**Performance Tiers (by profitability):**
- **Tier 1 (Highly Profitable):** 38 branches - Avg deposits $187M, 12,400 customers, ROAA 1.82%
- **Tier 2 (Moderately Profitable):** 55 branches - Avg deposits $94M, 6,800 customers, ROAA 0.74%
- **Tier 3 (Break-even):** 17 branches - Avg deposits $42M, 3,200 customers, ROAA 0.12%
- **Tier 4 (Unprofitable):** 17 branches - Avg deposits $28M, 1,900 customers, ROAA -0.38%

**Industry Benchmarks (Federal Reserve 2024):**
- Regional bank avg branches per $1B deposits: 3.2 (our current: 4.7)
- Cost-to-income ratio: Industry avg 62% (our current: 71%)
- Digital adoption rate: Industry avg 78% (our current: 64%)

## Recommended Actions

**Phase 1: Close 34 Branches (Q2-Q3 2026)**
- Tier 3 underperformers: 17 branches
- Tier 4 unprofitable: 17 branches
- Expected customer retention: 87% (migrate to nearest branch or digital)
- Annual cost savings: $18.7 million
- One-time closure costs: $4.2 million (lease terminations, severance)

**Phase 2: Renovate 18 Flagship Branches (Q4 2026-Q2 2027)**
- Interactive teller machines (ITMs) for 24/7 service
- Private wealth management consultation areas
- Community meeting spaces
- Digital experience centers
- Renovation cost: $420,000 per branch = $7.56 million total

**Phase 3: Open 8 New Growth Market Branches (2027-2028)**
- Target markets: Sunbelt growth cities (Austin, Charlotte, Nashville, Phoenix, Tampa, Raleigh, Boise, Tucson)
- Flagship format with full digital integration
- Cost per new branch: $1.8 million = $14.4 million total

## Financial Impact

**Total Investment:**
- Branch closures: $4.2M (one-time)
- Renovations: $7.56M
- New branches: $14.4M
- Technology upgrades: $3.8M
- **Total: $30M**

**3-Year Financial Projection:**
- Annual cost savings from closures: $18.7M
- Incremental revenue from renovations: $4.2M (improved customer experience, wealth management)
- Revenue from new branches: $12.8M (by Year 3)
- Net benefit Year 3: $35.7M annually
- Payback period: 10 months
- 3-year NPV: $94.2 million

## Competitive Positioning

**Peer Comparisons:**
- PNC Bank closed 160 branches in 2023-2024, invested $1.2B in technology
- U.S. Bank reduced footprint 22%, repositioned remaining branches as advisory centers
- Truist consolidated 37 branches post-merger, saved $240M annually
- Regional banks that maintained high branch counts: Decreased market cap by avg 18% vs. digital-optimized peers

## Customer Impact Mitigation

- 6-month notice for closures with proactive customer outreach
- Mobile branch units for underserved rural communities (4 units @ $180K each)
- Enhanced digital onboarding with live video banking
- Dedicated call center team for transitioning customers
- Expected customer attrition: 13% (industry avg 15-20% for branch closures)

## Workforce Impact

- 289 full-time equivalent positions eliminated
- 127 positions created (new flagship branches, digital roles)
- Net reduction: 162 FTE
- Severance and retraining program: $6.4 million
- Priority rehire for employees willing to relocate

## Timeline

- Q1 2026: Board approval and regulatory notifications
- Q2 2026: Customer communications, begin Tier 4 closures
- Q3 2026: Complete all closures, begin renovations
- Q4 2026-Q2 2027: Complete renovations
- Q3-Q4 2027: Open first 4 new flagship branches
- 2028: Open remaining 4 new branches

## Success Metrics

- Cost-to-income ratio: 71% → 58% (industry leading)
- Digital adoption: 64% → 84%
- Customer satisfaction: 4.1 → 4.6 stars
- Deposits per branch: $89M → $156M (75% increase)
- Revenue per branch: $2.4M → $3.8M (58% increase)
